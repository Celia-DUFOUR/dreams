♙ Our different Styles ♙

Breakdance

Characterized by its acrobatic movements, fluid footwork, and intricate spins, breakdancing is often performed to hip-hop, funk, or breakbeat music. Dancers, known as b-boys or b-girls, showcase their creativity, athleticism, and individual style through a combination of power moves, freezes, and intricate footwork patterns. Breakdance battles, where dancers compete against each other, are a common feature of the culture, with judges assessing technique, musicality, and originality. Beyond its roots in urban culture, breakdancing has evolved into a global phenomenon, influencing mainstream dance styles and continuing to captivate audiences with its electrifying performances.

Contemporary

Contemporary emphasizes freedom of expression, fluidity, and versatility, blending elements from various dance disciplines such as ballet, modern dance, and improvisation. Unlike classical ballet, contemporary dance often prioritizes individual creativity and experimentation over strict technique and formality. Dancers explore a wide range of movements, including floor work, release technique, and partnering, allowing them to convey emotions, narratives, and abstract concepts through their bodies. Contemporary dance performances can be deeply personal and reflective, pushing boundaries and challenging conventional notions of dance and movement.

Afro dance

Characterized by its infectious energy, syncopated rhythms, and joyful movements, Afro dance celebrates the rich cultural heritage of the African continent. Dancers often incorporate elements such as hip isolations, body rolls, and intricate footwork, drawing inspiration from diverse African dance traditions including West African, South African, and Afro-Caribbean styles. Afro dance is more than just a physical expression; it embodies a sense of community, spirituality, and storytelling, often conveying themes of celebration, resilience, and unity.

Hip-Hop

Hip hop dance, characterized by its energetic and improvisational style, includes a wide range of movements such as breaking, popping, locking, and krumping, and is often performed to hip hop music or its derivatives. Hip hop culture also encompasses graffiti art, DJing, MCing (rapping), and street fashion, reflecting the creativity, resilience, and expression of marginalized communities. Over the decades, hip hop has evolved into a global phenomenon, influencing mainstream culture and providing a platform for diverse voices to be heard and celebrated.

Latin dances

Latin couple dances encompass a diverse array of passionate and rhythmic dance styles originating from Latin America. These dances often emphasize the connection and chemistry between partners, with intricate footwork, sensual movements, and vibrant music setting the stage for dynamic performances. Popular Latin couple dances include salsa, bachata, merengue, and Argentine tango, each with its own unique characteristics and cultural influences.

Kpop

K-pop dances are a central element of the Korean pop music industry, known for their high energy, precision, and captivating choreography. Characterized by their intricate and synchronized movements, K-pop dances are carefully crafted to complement the music and enhance the overall performance. These dances often incorporate a mix of dance styles, including hip hop, jazz, contemporary, and traditional Korean dance, resulting in a dynamic fusion of cultural influences. Overall, K-pop dances are a vibrant and integral aspect of the genre, captivating fans around the world with their creativity, athleticism, and infectious energy.

Commercial dances

Commercial dance styles can vary widely, encompassing elements of hip hop, jazz, contemporary, ballet, and street dance, among others. Choreographers and dancers in the commercial dance industry often draw inspiration from popular culture, current trends, and mainstream music, creating dynamic and visually striking routines that resonate with audiences of all ages and backgrounds.In commercial dance, the emphasis is typically on polished technique, expressive performance, and engaging choreography that complements the music or concept of the production. These dances may incorporate elements of storytelling, character portrayal, or thematic imagery to enhance their impact and appeal to a broad audience. Heel dance is part of commercial dance.
